GenAI eTextbook Platform

The Problem

Educators spend significant time adapting academic resources for different audiences, learning needs, and contexts. The Dean of Science had an early-stage idea worth proving — but no spec, no team, and no defined scope, just a vision.

The Approach

A GenAI platform that transforms existing materials into audience-adaptive eTextbooks, powered by RAG, LLMs, and a multi-agent pipeline. An interactive editing layer keeps educators in control of their material throughout.

The Impact

A working 0→1 product built from an idea — with a clear vision, milestones, and a system that helps instructors reach diverse learners while reducing manual effort. Research from the build is feeding an academic paper on AI in higher education.

Results
  • Working GenAI platform with an interactive editing layer giving educators full control
  • 0 → 1: idea translated into product vision, scope, and a working system
  • 10+ faculty interviewed, insights directly shaping the product
  • Multi-agent RAG + LLM pipeline architected and iterated
  • Academic paper on AI in higher education (in progress)
  • Regular progress reviews with 3 senior academic stakeholders
My Role & Responsibilities
  • Translated the Dean’s concept into a product vision with defined scope and deliverables
  • Presented regular progress updates to 3 senior academic stakeholders
  • Designed and conducted user research with 10+ faculty on material-development workflows
  • Synthesised research into product decisions and an academic paper
  • Built and iterated on the multi-agent pipeline architecture
The product call

Rather than fully automating content generation, I prioritized an interactive editing layer that keeps educators in control. For adoption in academia, trust mattered more than automation for its own sake.
