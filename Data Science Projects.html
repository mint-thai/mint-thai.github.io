Churn & Segmentation · Course Project · 2025

Retail Customer Churn Analysis & Prediction

Analyzed 1M+ retail transactions to identify high-risk customer segments and predict customer churn.

Live Dashboard GitHub Repo Report (PDF) Notebook
★Executive SummaryIf you read one section, read this.
The whole project in brief

Retailers were losing revenue to churn without knowing which customers were leaving or why. Using 1M+ transactions from the Online Retail II dataset, I engineered RFM features, segmented customers with K-Means, and compared churn models. A Random Forest reached 80.5% F1 and flagged 69.7% of customers as at-risk — turning a vague "we're losing people" into a ranked, actionable retention list.

01Project Details
1 · Problem Statement

Customer churn directly impacts revenue and customer lifetime value. The challenge is identifying at-risk customers before they leave, so retention efforts can be targeted effectively instead of sprayed across the entire base. Retention and marketing teams are the ones who benefit from a clear, prioritized list.

2 · Objectives
  • Identify meaningful customer segments from raw transaction behavior.
  • Predict each customer's churn risk with a reliable model.
  • Understand which behaviors are most associated with churn.

7 · Key Findings
1
Finding

Random Forest clearly outperformed Logistic Regression on identifying churners.

Why it matters

A more reliable model means retention budget is spent on the right customers.

2
Finding

K-Means cleanly separated a high-value, recently-active segment from a low-value, lapsing one.

Why it matters

Segments let the business tailor messaging instead of treating everyone the same.

3
Finding

Recency and frequency were the strongest signals of churn risk.

Why it matters

Simple, trackable behaviors can trigger outreach without complex data.

8 · Recommendations
  • Focus retention campaigns on high-value customers with declining purchase frequency.
  • Trigger automated outreach after two consecutive inactive periods.

10 · Challenges & Lessons Learned

Class imbalance made model evaluation tricky — accuracy was misleading, so I leaned on F1. Next, I'd add a cost-aware decision threshold (weighing the cost of a retention offer against the value of a saved customer) and validate the churn flags against a real retention campaign to measure lift.
