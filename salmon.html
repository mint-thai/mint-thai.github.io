Mar 2025 – Present

AI-Powered Salmon Monitoring

The Problem

The SFC and SNR monitor salmon populations to guide conservation and community decisions. Their semi-automated process worked — but manual review and validation of footage could delay results by months, slowing the decisions that depend on them.

The Approach

An AI system that detects, tracks, and counts salmon straight from stream-camera footage. A custom CV model finds fish per frame, a tracking layer follows individuals to avoid double counts, and length is measured automatically — output that drops into partners’ existing workflows.

The Impact

Near real-time counts within 1–2% of manually verified truth, cutting processing time roughly in half — freeing the team from months of manual review and laying a foundation for future environmental-monitoring work.

Results
  • 98% mAP50 detection accuracy on evaluation footage
  • ~90% salmon counting accuracy
  • Counts within 1–2% of manually verified truth
  • ~50% faster processing → near real-time analysis
  • Foundation laid for future AI & environmental monitoring
  • Presented at the BC PARF Conference, May 2026
My Role & Responsibilities
  • Owned scope, roadmap, and stakeholder alignment end to end
  • Built and trained the detection + tracking pipeline
  • Validated counts frame-by-frame against manual truth data
  • Troubleshot a Linux deployment failure before a key milestone
  • Translated technical progress into plain-language partner updates
The trade-off

Optimizing the counting algorithm for rare edge cases made it worse on common ones. I made the deliberate call to prioritize overall accuracy — as long as results stayed within the agreed error margin. It was the right trade-off.

What’s Next
Going live

The system goes live on-site within weeks. We’ll monitor in the field, gather partner feedback, and keep improving the model.
